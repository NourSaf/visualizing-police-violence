The total number of cases from
September 1985 to January 2020
is 33,358.
Out of a total of 17,114 cases, 51.30%
involve individuals identified as Black.
72.12% of the cases are registered by males
15.05% of the cases are registered by Females
The main categories registered are Discourtesy with total of 4498 cases
and Force with total of 7563 cases
and Abuse of Authority with total of 19029 cases
There are over 115 allegations, with 15% involving physical force and 15% involving the use of offensive language.
67% of the cases are registered against police officers, 18% against sergeants, and 10% against detectives.

This scrollytelling project, Police Use of Power in New York,
explores civilian complaints against the NYPD from September 1985 to January 2020.
The data source is ProPublicaBy visualizing over 12,000 cases, the project sheds
light on patterns of misconduct,accountability, and the complex dynamics
of policing in New York City.

Developed as the final project for the Data Visualization and Information Aesthetics
course in Fall 2024, it combines narrative and data-driven insights to provoken
thought and foster awareness about the impact of institutional power. /span>
